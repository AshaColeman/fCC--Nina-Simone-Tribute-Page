The Life & Legacy of Nina Simone

Timeline

  • 1933: On February 21st @ 6am, Eunice Kathleen Waymon is born in Tryon, NC
  • 1938: Eunice became the official pianist of the Methodist Chapel of Tryon and began to attend school for black children
  • 1939: Mrs. Miller pays Eunice's first piano lesson with Mrs. Muriel Mazzanovich ("Miss Mazzy") wife of artist Lawrence Mazzanovich (1872-1959)
  • 1943: Eunice, age 10, gave her first piano recital at the Town Hall in Tryon
  • 1950: Eunice, age 17, attends Juilliard School of Music, New York, NY, to prepare for Curtis Institute of Music, Philadelphia, PA; She was rejected by Curtis Institute later that year after family had already moved to Philadelphia
  • 1952: Eunice worked as a pianist for Arlene Smith, a voice teacher; takes piano lessons with Vladimir Sokoloff (1913-1997)
  • 1954: Eunice takes a job as singer-pianist in Atlantic City, NJ at the Midtown Bar and Grill and decides to use the stage name Nina Simone so her mother wouldn't find out
  • 1955: Nina returns in Atlantic City, NJ at Midtown Bar and Grill. Meets Ted Axelrod
  • 1957: First recording session with Bethlehem (13 hours, 14 songs). Precise date is not known, but it was in the late fall (December?)
    • 1958: Marries Don Ross in Philadelphia
  • 1959: Nina moves to New York
  • 1959: (April) Nina signs with Colpix, a division of Columbia Pictures
  • 1959: (September) Debut concert at Town Hall, NY
  • 1960: Nina and Don Ross get divorced
  • 1961: Nina meets and marries Andy Stroud
  • 1961: (December) Ten days in Lagos (Nigeria) for a series of concerts with Randy Weston, Langston Hughes, James Baldwin, Lionel Hampton under AMSAC (American Society of Africal Culture) sign
  • 1962: Andy Stroud becomes Nina's manager; later that year Lisa Celeste Stroud is born
  • 1962:Nina befriends noted playwright Lorraine Hansberry and spoke often with her about the Civil Rights Movement
  • 1963: Nina met with other activist-performers at Theodore Bikel's Greenwich Village home
  • 1963: (September) Birmingham, AL murder of four black school children who were attending a Bible study class in 16th Street Baptist Church; in the disturbances that followed, two more black people were killed; Nina composes the political anthem "Mississippi Goddam"
  • 1965: Lorraine Hansberry, Nina's intellectual mentor, died from cancer at 35; Nina takes the title of the play Lorraine was working on, Young, Gifted and Black and turned it into a black anthem
  • 1967:First signs of Bipolar Disorder appeared
  • 1967:Nina is "Female Jazz Singer of the Year" at the annual convention of NATRA (National Association of Television and Radio Announcers, 9-12 August) in Atlanta, GA
  • 1969:Nina and Andy seperate
  • 1971:Filed for divorce from husband/manager Andy Stroud (finalized in 1972); Sam Waymon (Nina's brother) becomes her manager; Nina moves to Barbados
  • 1974:Nina leaves Barbados, moves to New York
  • 1976:Nina leaves Liberia, moves to Switzerland (Prangin)
  • 1977:Amherst College (Massachusetts, US) presented Nina with an honorary doctor of Music degree; Nina begins calling herself "Dr. Nina Simone"
  • 1978:Arrested for withholding taxes in 1971-73; later pleads guilty
  • 1981:Nina meets Rayond Gonzalez
  • 1982: Nina leaves Geneva (CH) and moves to Paris (FR) where she rented a small apartment
  • 1984:Leaves UK and moves to Trinidad
  • 1985:Moves to Holland, close to the German border
  • 1986:Nina moves to Amsterdam
  • 1988:Raymond Gonzalez becomes Nina's manager
  • 1990:Nina is awarded the Grand Prix of Académie Charles-Cros, Paris
  • 1991:Autobiography I Put a Spell on You (with Stephen Cleary) released by Pantheon Books, New York
  • 1992:Nina moves to Bouc-Bel-Air near Aix-en-Provence (France)
  • 1995:Nina was awarded ownership of 52 of her master recordings (five full-legth albums) by a federal judge in San Francisco. San Juan Music Group and others were enjoined from manufacturing Simone's material without her consent
  • 1998:Nina becomes Honorary Ambassador of Côte d'Ivoire
  • 1999:Receives a Lifetime Achievement in Music Award
  • 2000:Inducted in the Grammy Hall of Fame for "I Loves You Porgy" (Bethlehem 1959), received Diamond Award for Excellence in Music from the Association of African American Music, and the Honorable Musketeer Award from the Compagnie des Mousquetaires d'Armagnac
  • 2002:Concert in Sopot (PL) at Hestia (Nina's last performance)
  • 2003:On April 21st, Nina Simone died in her sleep at her home in Carry-le-Rouet, France at 70 years old

Obituary by Roger Nupie, International Dr. Nina Simone Fan Club

With inconsolable sadness we let you know that Dr. NINA SIMONE has passed away. We understand this news will come as a shock to the whole world and the millions who love her music. We are sure she will forever be remembered for her exceptional music, for her genius, her talent, her courage, her fight for racial justice and her commitment to the Civil Rights Movement. She will forever be remembered as the ultimate songstress and storyteller of our times. We give thanks to the DIVA for sharing almost 50 years of music with us. We give thanks to Dr. NINA SIMONE for the beauty, consolation and strength her voice, piano and songs will give until the end of time to people, regardless of age, race and culture. You and your music will be with us every day of our life, in our heart, in our soul, in our thoughts. In our mind. May you rest in peace, dearest NINA. God bless you.
